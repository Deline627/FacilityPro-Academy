Stage sequence: Kitchens → Bathrooms → Glass → Mopping
8-step mopping procedure — final task in the wet workflow, every time, no exceptions

WET FLOOR SAFETY STANDARD — Place wet floor signs BEFORE mopping. Remove ONLY when fully dry. No exceptions.
PREP
1
Confirm all prior wet stages are complete
Kitchens, bathrooms, and glass must be fully signed off before mopping begins. Do not mop any area that has not been cleaned and verified. Mopping is always the final task.
2
Prepare mop equipment and floor cleaner
Fill mop bucket with correct pre-filled floor cleaner. Confirm mop head is clean and in good condition. Place wet floor signs at all entry points to the area being mopped before any mopping begins.
CLEAN
3
Mop all hard floors — back-to-front using figure-8 motion
Begin at the far end of the space and work toward the exit. Use overlapping figure-8 passes for maximum coverage. Maintain consistent pressure and overlapping passes — do not leave gaps. Do not re-enter mopped areas.
4
Rinse mop head every 2–3 passes
Rinse mop head in clean water regularly. A dirty mop spreads contamination — not cleaning. If water becomes dirty, STOP and change immediately. Do not continue mopping with soiled water.
5
Change mop water between zones
Change mop water between kitchens, washrooms, and general floor areas. Do not carry contaminated water between zones. Refill with correct pre-filled floor cleaner each time.
Do NOT mop any area until vacuuming and all surface cleaning is complete
FINISH
6
Inspect all mopped floors for streaks, pooling, or missed areas
Walk the perimeter of each mopped area. Check for visible mop streaks, pooling water, or missed sections. Check edges and corners for missed areas. Re-mop any area that does not meet standard.
7
Confirm wet floor signs are placed and visible from all entry points
All wet floor signs must remain in place until floors are completely dry. Do not remove signs early. Ensure signs are visible from ALL entry points — not just the main entrance. This is a safety requirement.
8
Restore layout, complete final verification, and sign off
Return all items to original positions. Confirm no equipment left in mopped areas. Conduct final visual inspection under available light. Sign off mopping zone — this is the final task before Stage 5.
Non-negotiable rules
• Mop is ALWAYS the final task — no exceptions
• Do NOT mop before vacuuming is complete
• Do NOT re-enter mopped areas
• Change mop water between zones
• Wet floor signs placed BEFORE mopping begins
• Do NOT remove signs until floor is fully dry
• Do NOT use dirty or contaminated mop water
• Work back-to-front toward exit — always
• Do NOT leave mop streaks or uneven finish
• Do NOT mop over areas not yet vacuumed
Final verification — before leaving mopped area
All floors mopped — no missed areas
No visible mop streaks or residue
No pooling water anywhere
Floor drying evenly — uniform appearance
No footprints or re-entry marks on mopped areas
Mop water changed between zones
Wet floor signs in place and visible from all entry points
No equipment left in mopped areas
Floor passes visual inspection under light
All items returned to correct position
Wet floor signs removed only after floor is fully dry
