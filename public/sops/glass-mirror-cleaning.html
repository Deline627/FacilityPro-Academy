STAGE 4 — SERVICE DELIVERY

Wet workflow — Glass SOP

Stage sequence: Kitchens → Bathrooms → Glass → Mopping
8-step glass procedure — execute in order, every time, no exceptions
WHITE cloth only
FacilityPro Academy | v4.0
Laminate and display on site
NO STREAKS STANDARD — Glass must pass visual inspection at 45° angle under available light
PREP
1
Clear access to all glass surfaces
Remove all obstacles from in front of glass panels, partitions, windows, and mirrors. Ensure full visibility and unobstructed access to every glass surface in scope.
2
Prepare glass cleaning tools WHITE cloth
Confirm WHITE microfibre glass cloth is clean and dry. Prepare glass cleaner spray bottle. Prepare squeegee if required for large panels. Do not use cloths from any other zone.
CLEAN
3
Apply glass cleaner to surface WHITE cloth
Spray glass cleaner sparingly — do not oversaturate. Excess spray creates streaks and overspray onto surrounding surfaces. One to two sprays per panel is sufficient.
4
Wipe glass in consistent vertical passes — do not change direction mid-clean
Wipe in overlapping vertical passes from top to bottom. Do not change direction mid-clean — inconsistent patterns leave visible streaks. Cover the full surface including edges.
5
Buff glass to a completely dry finish — no moisture, streaks, or lint
Using a dry section of the WHITE cloth, buff the entire surface to remove all moisture, streaks, and smears. Glass must be completely dry — no residue, no lint, no marks.
Complete all glass before moving to mopping stage
FINISH
6
Detail all edges, corners, and frames
Check and wipe all glass edges, corners, frames, and handles for residue, cleaner drips, or overspray. Edges are where streaks collect — do not skip them.
7
Inspect glass at 45° angle under available light
Step to the side and view every glass surface at a 45-degree angle. Check under natural or artificial light for streaks, smears, fingerprints, or lint. Re-clean any surface that does not pass.
8
Restore layout, complete final verification, and sign off
Return all items moved during Step 1 to original positions. Confirm no overspray on surrounding surfaces. Conduct final visual check. Sign off glass zone before proceeding to mopping.
Non-negotiable rules
• WHITE cloth only for all glass surfaces
• Do NOT use dirty or damp cloths
• Do NOT overspray cleaner
• Always buff to a completely dry finish
• Clean edges and frames — not just glass centre
• Do NOT leave streaks, smears, or lint
• Replace cloth if it becomes damp or dirty
• Do NOT clean glass with a damp or saturated cloth
• Do NOT use glass spray on anti-glare screens
Final verification — before leaving glass zone
Glass streak-free under direct light
No smears or fingerprints visible
Edges and corners clean — no residue
Frames and handles wiped
No lint or cloth residue on any surface
Glass passes inspection at 45° angle
No streaks visible from multiple viewing angles
Surrounding surfaces not affected by overspray
Area left clean and dry
